Inchcape Singapore announces 2 years partnership with Singapore Polytechnic to turbocharge the electric vehicle (EV) readiness of the community.

Inchcape Singapore announces landmark partnership with Singapore Polytechnic to fast-track the future of sustainable mobility. Inchcape Singapore and Singapore Polytechnic had also ink a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) that marks a strategic collaboration that will see the progressive roll-out of educational experiences to future-proof local talents' technological and digital capabilities in Electronic Vehicles.

Singapore Polytechnic and Inchcape will be co-designing and co-developing advanced EV training curriculum on design, assembly, system modelling and simulation for learners. This will cover present and future mobility solutions and include infrastructure, technology, capabilities and resources on EV.

The MoU acts as a catalyst to strengthen EV readiness in Singapore by preparing next-gen leaders and talents for the future of mobility through education. This strategic partnership aligns both with Inchcape Singapore and SP's commitment to driving sustainability efforts, in line with the SG GREEN PLAN 2030 as well as future-proofing and building the EV capabilities of Singapore's talent pool.

By 2024, approximately 700 learners would be equipped with the knowledge necessary to drive the future of mobility.

TIE Wins Gold Award at the 2021 MOE Inergy Awards

The majority of end-of-life or defective photovoltaic (PV) modules often end up in landfills, which can pose a potential environmental hazard due to the leaching of toxic heavy metals, such as lead and silver, into groundwater. Furthermore, existing methods for recycling PV modules are frequently unsuitable and inefficient.

The department for Technology, Innovation, and Enterprise at SP has successfully developed a patented technology that allows for the recovery of all valuable materials from crystalline silicon PV modules using environmentally-friendly processes. With this innovative approach, the PV panels are not crushed, enabling the recovery of the glass intact, while the silicon substrate can be retrieved in separate pieces instead of being reduced to a powdered mixture. Additionally, all valuable substances extracted from the PV modules become high-value products, and the proprietary etchants used are gentle and can be reused for multiple extraction batches.
